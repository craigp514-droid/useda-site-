# US Expansion Planning Checklist

**A comprehensive guide to everything you need to consider before selecting a US location**

*Provided by the US Economic Development Association*

---

## How to Use This Checklist

This checklist is designed to help you think through the key considerations for your US expansion before you start evaluating specific locations. Working through these questions will help you:

- Define your project requirements clearly
- Identify information gaps early
- Have productive conversations with economic development organizations and site selectors
- Avoid costly surprises later in the process

---

- [ ] **Space requirements:**
  - [ ] Square footage needed: _______

### Timeline Questions

- [ ] When do you need to be operational? _______

## Section 4: Cost Considerations

### One-Time Costs

- [ ] Land acquisition or lease deposits
- [ ] Building construction or upfit
- [ ] Equipment and machinery
- [ ] Furniture, fixtures, and equipment
- [ ] Relocation costs (employees, equipment)
- [ ] Legal and professional fees
- [ ] Permitting and impact fees
- [ ] Utility connection fees

### Ongoing Operating Costs

- [ ] **Labor costs:**
  - [ ] Wages and salaries
  - [ ] Benefits (healthcare, retirement, etc.)
  - [ ] Payroll taxes
  - [ ] Workers' compensation insurance

- [ ] **Facility costs:**
  - [ ] Lease/mortgage payments
  - [ ] Property taxes
  - [ ] Property insurance
  - [ ] Maintenance and repairs

- [ ] **Utility costs:**
  - [ ] Electricity
  - [ ] Natural gas
  - [ ] Water and sewer
  - [ ] Telecommunications

- [ ] **Taxes:**
  - [ ] Corporate income tax (state)
  - [ ] Sales and use tax
  - [ ] Property tax
  - [ ] Franchise/gross receipts tax
  - [ ] Unemployment insurance tax

- [ ] **Transportation/logistics costs:**
  - [ ] Inbound freight
  - [ ] Outbound freight
  - [ ] Distribution costs

### Cost Comparison Framework

When evaluating locations, compare the total cost of operation over 10 years, not just the upfront costs or incentives. The lowest-cost site in year one may not be the lowest-cost site over the life of the project.

## Section 5: Regulatory & Compliance

### Environmental Considerations

- [ ] Will your operation require environmental permits?
- [ ] What emissions will your facility produce? (air, water, waste)
- [ ] Are there hazardous materials involved?
- [ ] Will you need an Environmental Impact Assessment?
- [ ] Do you have specific sustainability or ESG commitments?

### Zoning & Land Use

- [ ] What zoning classification do you need?
- [ ] Are there height, setback, or coverage restrictions to consider?
- [ ] Will you need any variances or special permits?
- [ ] Are there design review or architectural requirements?

### Building & Construction

- [ ] What building codes apply?
- [ ] What permits will be required?
- [ ] What inspections will be needed?
- [ ] What is the typical permitting timeline in your target areas?

### Business Licensing

- [ ] What state business registrations are required?
- [ ] What local business licenses are needed?
- [ ] Are there industry-specific licenses or certifications required?

### Employment Law

- [ ] Understand state-specific employment laws:
  - [ ] Minimum wage requirements
  - [ ] Overtime rules
  - [ ] Leave requirements (sick, family, etc.)
  - [ ] Non-compete enforceability
  - [ ] Right-to-work status
  - [ ] At-will employment rules

## Section 6: Incentives & Assistance

### Types of Incentives to Explore

- [ ] **Tax incentives:**
  - [ ] Corporate income tax credits or exemptions
  - [ ] Sales tax exemptions (equipment, materials)
  - [ ] Property tax abatements or exemptions
  - [ ] Job creation tax credits
  - [ ] Investment tax credits

- [ ] **Cash incentives:**
  - [ ] Cash grants
  - [ ] Forgivable loans
  - [ ] Performance-based rebates

- [ ] **Financing incentives:**
  - [ ] Low-interest loans
  - [ ] Bond financing
  - [ ] Loan guarantees

- [ ] **Real estate incentives:**
  - [ ] Free or reduced-cost land
  - [ ] Building grants or loans
  - [ ] Infrastructure improvements

- [ ] **Workforce incentives:**
  - [ ] Training grants
  - [ ] Customized training programs
  - [ ] Recruitment assistance
  - [ ] Wage subsidies

- [ ] **Utility incentives:**
  - [ ] Reduced utility rates
  - [ ] Connection fee waivers
  - [ ] Infrastructure extensions

### Incentive Considerations

- [ ] What are the requirements to qualify? (jobs, wages, investment thresholds)
- [ ] What are the clawback provisions if you don't meet commitments?
- [ ] What reporting requirements exist?
- [ ] How long do the incentives last?
- [ ] When are incentives paid? (upfront vs. over time)

### Important Note on Incentives

Incentives should not drive your site selection decisionâ€”they should be the final factor after you've identified locations that meet your operational requirements. A location with great incentives but poor workforce availability or high operating costs is not a good deal.

- [ ] Climate preferences
