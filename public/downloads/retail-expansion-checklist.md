**Your guide to establishing retail operations in the United States**

*Provided by the US Economic Development Association*

## Overview

Entering the US retail market—whether as an international brand opening your first location or expanding an existing footprint—requires careful analysis of consumer behavior, site selection, and operational considerations unique to the American market. This checklist will help you navigate the complexity.

## 9. US Market Entry: Key Differences

### What International Brands Should Know

**Consumer Expectations:**
- [ ] Return policies are very generous in US (30-90 days common)
- [ ] Customer service expectations are high
- [ ] Credit card usage is dominant
- [ ] "Sales" and promotional pricing are expected
- [ ] Sizing and measurement differences (US vs. metric)

**Operational Differences:**
- [ ] Tipping culture (not retail, but service-adjacent)
- [ ] Health insurance is employer-provided
- [ ] Employment "at-will" in most states
- [ ] Workers' compensation insurance required
- [ ] Litigation environment is more active

**Marketing Considerations:**
- [ ] Social media platforms differ (US = Instagram, TikTok, Facebook)
- [ ] Influencer marketing landscape
- [ ] Holiday calendar differs (Black Friday, Memorial Day, etc.)
- [ ] Regional marketing may be needed (US is not homogeneous)

## 10. Financial Incentives

### Retail-Specific Incentives

Retail projects typically receive fewer incentives than manufacturing, but opportunities exist:

- [ ] **Sales tax rebates** (some states offer for major retailers)
- [ ] **Job creation credits** (if significant employment)
- [ ] **Facade improvement grants** (downtown/main street programs)
- [ ] **TIF districts** (tax increment financing areas)
- [ ] **Enterprise/opportunity zones** (tax benefits in designated areas)
- [ ] **New market tax credits** (for underserved areas)

### When Incentives Are More Likely

- [ ] Flagship/destination retail
- [ ] Significant job creation (100+ jobs)
- [ ] Downtown/urban revitalization areas
- [ ] Anchor tenant in new development
- [ ] Underserved/food desert areas (grocery)

## 13. Next Steps

### Recommended Approach

1. **Define your US market strategy** using this checklist
2. **Conduct consumer research** to validate target market
3. **Identify priority markets** for entry (2-3 metros)
4. **Engage retail real estate broker** in target markets
5. **Conduct site tours** and evaluate specific locations
6. **Negotiate LOI** for preferred site(s)
7. **Finalize legal and operational setup**
